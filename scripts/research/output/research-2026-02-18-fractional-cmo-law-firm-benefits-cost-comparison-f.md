#### The Cost Advantage: Fractional vs Full-Time CMO Investment
- Full-time CMO salary ranges $150K-$300K+ annually plus benefits; fractional costs $5K-$15K monthly ($60K-$180K annually)
- Fractional model delivers 40-60% cost savings while providing senior-level expertise without long-term employment risk
- Law firms pay only for hours needed (typically 10-20 hours weekly) rather than supporting full-time overhead

#### Measurable ROI: 29% Growth Differential in 2025-2026
- Companies with fractional CMOs achieved 29% average revenue growth versus 19% without fractional leadership (10-point differential)
- Growth compounds through 'compounding assets': content libraries, attorney bios, practice-area authority, and review velocity
- 54% of legal marketing decision-makers increased budgets recently but need strategic governance to convert spend into signed cases

#### Why 2026 Demands Fractional Leadership Over Agencies
- Generative engine optimization and AI-driven search require bar-compliant messaging that generic agencies miss
- Fractional CMOs act as governance layer between firm, multiple vendors, and internal teams—solving coordination failures agencies can't address
- Fractional model links intake-to-revenue attribution directly, preventing budget waste when practice areas reach capacity

#### Three Conditions That Determine Fractional CMO Success
- Clear intake capacity planning: Define how many cases each practice area can handle before engaging fractional leader on demand generation
- Internal execution support: Firms lacking marketing staff or structured processes see limited ROI; fractional leader becomes bottleneck on execution, not strategy
- Weekly accountability structure: Establish KPI reviews, decision gates, and attribution models; advisory-only relationships fail to drive business impact

#### The Fractional Model Fails When Law Firms Make These Five Mistakes
- Treating fractional CMO as cost-reduction play instead of strategic governance tool—wrong hire for execution-only needs
- Expecting 30-day revenue impact when compounding assets require 90+ days to strengthen performance
- Continuing demand generation without capacity alignment—wastes budget and damages service quality
- Skipping legal-industry expertise requirements—generic CMOs lack bar-compliance and ethical advertising knowledge
- Neglecting weekly governance: Fractional engagement devolves into advisory calls rather than integrated leadership

#### The 90-Day Pilot Framework: Test Before Committing
- Define three specific pilot objectives: establish intake attribution model, audit vendor performance, design quarterly budget strategy
- Measure success against practice-area capacity (not just top-of-funnel metrics) to validate true ROI
- Decide scale/hold/stop at day 90 based on execution quality, team alignment, and attribution clarity—not vanity metrics
